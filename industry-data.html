General Skills

Upon analysis of what falls under general skills in the Burning Glass data, there are quite a few skills which overlap or that the team already utilises in their day to day jobs. The team already holds a large number of these requirements, indicating an ability to dedicate more time to achieving their IT-driven skills.

Review

Overall, the team at IDK-5 has a vast number of required skills both generally and within the IT-specific range; however, there are, of course, some missing. The IT industry is vast and not every skill in it is documented within this data, as previously discussed. Several IT skills are niche requirements for Larissa and James' ideal jobs while several skills in the general skills field either overlap or deemed common sense. In terms of IT-specific skills, the three highest-ranked skills that our team did not possess was Structure Query Language (SQL), SAP and graphic design. These skills, when ranked out of the twenty-five listed, came in at first, fifth and tenth respectively. While the direction of one's career is entirely up to them, this has made our group reconsider what paths to take in their future in order to make themselves as employable as possible. Throughout our assignment and in conjunction with a review of our team's collective skills, it has become quite clear that we are lack of creativity and decision making, which are ranked ninth and twenty-fifth, respectively. While the team is aware, the assessment requested our three missing skills; collectively, the team has agreed that the remainder of the skills are either common sense or overlapping with our previously attained skills.
